Conditions Treated

Start with your diagnosis

Endometriosis & Pelvic Pain

Endometriosis occurs when tissue similar to the lining of the uterus grows outside it, commonly causing pelvic pain, painful periods, and sometimes difficulty conceiving. Pelvic pain has many possible causes and deserves thorough, unhurried assessment.

[Stage 5 — expand: diagnosis approach, treatment options overview.]

Related procedures:Laparoscopic excision of endometriosis

Fibroids

Fibroids are non-cancerous growths of the uterus. Many cause no symptoms; others cause heavy bleeding, pressure symptoms or pain. Treatment ranges from monitoring to medication to surgery.

[Stage 5 — expand.]

Related procedures:MyomectomyHysterectomy

Abnormal Bleeding

Heavy, irregular, or postmenopausal bleeding warrants proper investigation. Dr Robertson provides thorough assessment to identify the cause and the full range of treatment options.

[Stage 5 — expand.]

Related procedures:HysteroscopyHysterectomy

Ovarian Cysts

Ovarian cysts are common and often resolve on their own, but some require specialist assessment or surgical management — always with careful attention to ovarian function and fertility.

[Stage 5 — expand.]

Related procedures:Ovarian surgery

Abnormal Cervical Screening Tests

An abnormal cervical screening result can be worrying. Dr Robertson provides assessment, colposcopy where indicated, and clear guidance on what your result means and what happens next.

[Stage 5 — expand.]

Related procedures:Cervical surgery

Vulval Disorders

Vulval conditions — including skin disorders and persistent discomfort — are common and treatable, and deserve sensitive, specialist care.

Procedures Performed

Know your operation?

Laparoscopic Excision of Endometriosis

Keyhole surgery to remove (excise) endometriosis tissue, performed through small incisions with the aim of treating disease thoroughly while preserving healthy tissue.

Related conditions:Endometriosis & pelvic pain

Hysterectomy (Removal of the Uterus)

Surgical removal of the uterus, performed minimally invasively where possible. A significant decision made together, when it is the right option for you.

Related conditions:FibroidsAbnormal bleeding

Ovarian Surgery

Surgery for ovarian cysts and other ovarian conditions, with a focus on preserving ovarian function wherever possible.

Related conditions:Ovarian cysts

Myomectomy (Removal of Fibroids)

Surgical removal of fibroids while preserving the uterus — often relevant for women who wish to retain fertility.

Related conditions:Fibroids

Hysteroscopy

A procedure to examine the inside of the uterus using a fine telescope, used to investigate and often treat abnormal bleeding and other conditions.

Related conditions:Abnormal bleeding

Cervical Surgery

Procedures of the cervix, including treatment following abnormal cervical screening results.

Related conditions:Abnormal cervical screening

Surgery for Infertility

Surgical assessment and treatment of conditions that can affect fertility, including endometriosis and uterine conditions.

Related conditions:Endometriosis & pelvic pain

Every procedure carries individual considerations. Benefits, risks and alternatives are discussed in full at your consultation.
