Becoming a Patient

The referral process

1

See your GP

A GP referral is required for all appointments. Your GP can send it electronically via HealthLink (kkarthig) or by email to info@drpipparobertson.com.

2

Referral review

Dr Robertson reviews and triages every referral personally, prioritised on clinical need.

3

We contact you

Once your referral has been triaged, our team will contact you to arrange your appointment.

4

Your first consultation

Held at our rooms — Suite 32, Hollywood Medical Centre, 85 Monash Avenue, Nedlands.

Please bring

  • Your referral (if not already sent)
  • Medicare card and private health insurance details
  • Any relevant imaging, results or reports
  • A list of current medications
  • Your questions — write them down beforehand
Dr Robertson is a private specialist — private health insurance is required. Fees are discussed prior to your consultation. [Stage 5 — confirm fee wording.]

FAQ

Common questions

Do I need a referral?

Yes — a current GP referral is required for all appointments, both for Medicare purposes and so Dr Robertson can review your history before you attend.

Can I be seen without private health insurance?

Dr Robertson consults privately and private health insurance is required. Your GP can discuss public system options with you if this isn't possible.

How long will I wait for an appointment?

Referrals are triaged on clinical need, so waiting times vary. Our team will give you an indication when we contact you. [Stage 5 — confirm wording.]

What happens after my first appointment?

That depends entirely on your situation — it may involve investigations, a treatment plan, or scheduling a procedure. Every next step is discussed with you before it's taken.
